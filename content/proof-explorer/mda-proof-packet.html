Seven-Question Classification

The 7Q Engine applied to the series thesis. Forward classification: what does the series claim at each level?

Q0 · Posture
Posture: human-led, evidence-seeking, with personal/testimonial motive separated from proof claims.
Q1 · Existence
Object: measurable change in American social/moral coherence across 1900-2025.
Q2 · Knowledge
Scope: MDA series claims are bounded to observed institutional, family, economic, technological, semantic, and religious indicators.
Q3 · Mechanism
Mechanism: coherence loss, feedback-loop collapse, semantic thinning, and domain-coupled social phase transition.
Q4 · Evidence
Evidence: grader/NLP outputs, markdown companions, source ledgers, timeline workbooks, citations, and article-level scorecards.
Q5 · Implications
Implications: proof layer should expose claims, citations, equations, and kill conditions without forcing every narrative sentence into proof status.
Q6 · Falsification
Falsification: competing model with stronger predictive accuracy, fewer parameters, different mechanism, or domain-level countertrend breaks the claim.
Q7 · Residue
Residue: theological interpretation and personal calling remain labeled, not smuggled in as empirical proof.
